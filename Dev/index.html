Carver Grange of Omaha (CarGO)

To bring together resources to develop and support family and community through education, leadership, advocacy, and economic development related to food.

On January 3, 2012, nineteen individuals became charter members of Carver Grange of Omaha. This charter represented the first new Nebraska chapter in 100 years.

To commemorate this event, on Jaunary 3, 2012, Ed Luttrell, Washington, DC, President, National Grange of the Order of Patrons and husbandry, traveled to Omaha to personally serve the group and its charter. The primary goal of the Carver Grange of Omaha is to expand hand-on applied STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Math) education and develop transferable leadership skills in youth while cultivating an interest in food and related agriculture education opportunities and careers.

Carver Grange of Omaha received 501(c)3 tax status December 2014.

OUR MISSION:To bring together resources to develop and support family and community through education, leadership, advocacy, and economic development related to food, agriculture, and natural resources.

OUR VISION:CarGo collaborates to make husbandry relevant in modern and urban society.

CORE VALUES:Education, Leadership Development, Community Engagement, Husbandry.
