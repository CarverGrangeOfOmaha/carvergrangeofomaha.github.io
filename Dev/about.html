On January 3, 2012, nineteen individuals became charter members of Carver Grange of Omaha. This charter represented the first new Nebraska chapter in 100 years. To commemorate this event, on Jaunary 3, 2012, Ed Luttrell, Washington, DC, President, National Grange of the Order of Patrons and Husbundry, traveled to Omaha to personally serve the group and its charter. The primary goal of the Carver Grange of Omaha is to expand hand-on applied STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Math) education and develop transferable leadership skills in youth while cultivating an interest in food and related agriculture education opportunities and careers.
