Carver Grange of Omaha (CarGO)

To bring together resources to develop and support family and community through education, leadership, advocacy, and economic development related to food.

On October 11, 2011, nineteen individuals attended the initial meeting to learn about the project, Carver Grange of Omaha. These individuals became the charter members. And then on January 3, 2012, Ed Luttrell, Washington, DC, President, National Grange of the Order of Patrons of Husbandry, flew in to serve the group its charter membership. This charter represented the first new Nebraska chapter in 100 years.

The primary goal of the Carver Grange of Omaha is to expand hand-on applied STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Math) education and develop transferable leadership skills in youth while cultivating an interest in food and related agriculture education opportunities and careers.

Carver Grange of Omaha received 501(c)3 tax status December 2014.

OUR MISSION: To bring together resources to develop and support family and community through education, leadership, advocacy, and economic development related to food, agriculture, and natural resources.

OUR VISION: CarGo collaborates to make husbandry relevant in modern and urban society.

CORE VALUES: Education, Leadership Development, Community Engagement, Husbandry.

PROJECTS, ACTIVITIES, and EVENTS:

George Washington Carver Grange 4-H Science Club will introduce youth ages 8-12 years to integrated hands-on applied Science, Technology, Engineering and Math (STEM) focused program in an out-of-school experience to help facilitate the education and development of transferable leadership skills in youth while cultivating an interest in food and agriculture. CarGO 4-H Club is held at OPS Druid Hill Elementary School.

Aksarben River City Shadow Camp is for urban youth ages 9 to 12 to meet and shadow 4-H Livestock Exhibitors and learn about their livestock projects. Sponsored Shadow Camp program with Aksarben River City Roundup.

The Dictionary Project is a project that provides a dictionary to every student across the United States to help them read and write. The National Grange became involved with The Dictionary Project, seven years ago; over 465,000 dictionaries have been donated to student by Grange members in 38 states. In 2012, CarGO provided the first set of dictionaries to fourth-grade classes at Druid Hill Elemntary in the Omaha Public School District.

Cooking Demonstration provided a community cooking demonstration using Grange recipes and facilitated a historical Grange discussion.
